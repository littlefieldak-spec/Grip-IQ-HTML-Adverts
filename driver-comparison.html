Pulse throttleRoot cause

The chain is unambiguous: Driver A's pulse-throttle application at exit spins the rear tire against the track surface, heating the RR by +5°F vs Driver B. That added heat reduces RR grip, which shows up as loose-on-exit events (6.5% vs 3.0%), which in turn forces more steering correction (28.5° vs 21.7°), which heats the RF. The whole cycle compounds across a run — which is why Driver A fades +1.85s while Driver B only fades +1.59s.

How to close
the gap.

Zone-by-zone coaching with specific input targets. Not "try harder" — exact numbers comparing what each driver does at every corner phase, with severity ratings and measurement targets.

Recommendations for Driver A
Closing a ~0.26s total gap to B over 49 laps · ~5ms/lap

1. T1&T2 — where most of the time is lost to the faster run

On entry, try ease onto the brake more gradually (7.2%/s vs 16.1%/s), brake a bit sooner (9.6% vs 12.2% of corner), use less brake (0.7% vs 3.0%), carry more speed (152.5 vs 151.0 MPH).

On centre, try carry more speed (150.9 vs 149.3 MPH).

On exit, try apply throttle more smoothly, commit to full throttle a bit later, carry less speed (151.0 vs 151.5 MPH).

These inputs are contributing to higher front tire temps at T1&T2 (201°F vs 199°F) and higher rear tire temps at T1&T2 (211°F vs 204°F). Front temps rise 44°F from Q1 to Q4 — reducing the aggressive inputs will help preserve tires.

2. T3&T4 — less time was lost here, but the biggest driving-style differences were identified

On entry, try ease onto the brake more gradually (10.1%/s vs 20.5%/s), use less brake (28.6% vs 31.4%), carry less speed (139.6 vs 141.6 MPH).

On centre, try carry a bit more throttle (48% vs 42%), carry less speed (130.7 vs 131.1 MPH), use less wheel (39.0° vs 41.9°).

On exit, try carry a bit more throttle (86% vs 81%), apply throttle more smoothly, commit to full throttle a bit later, carry more speed (133.3 vs 132.0 MPH).

These inputs are contributing to higher front tire temps at T3&T4 (209°F vs 201°F) and higher rear tire temps at T3&T4 (212°F vs 207°F). Front temps rise 28°F from Q1 to Q4 — reducing the aggressive inputs will help preserve tires.

Bottom line: Driver A fades +1.85s vs Driver B's +1.59s. The tire findings above (front heat — front tires are sliding and scrubbing more · rear heat — the rear tire is spinning faster than the car is moving) explain why — fixing the inputs will fix the fade.
