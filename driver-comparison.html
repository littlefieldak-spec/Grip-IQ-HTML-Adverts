Detailed Coaching Report

Driver profiles: Pro is the “tire preserver” — slower early but sustainable — preserves the tires and maintains pace late in the run when it matters most. HL Hero is the “early burner” — fast early but fades — burns through the tires with aggressive inputs, building a lead that evaporates as the run progresses.

Recommendations for HL Hero (closing a +1.111s total gap to Pro over 25 laps — +0.044s/lap):

The story of the run: HL Hero is faster early (+0.249s/lap in Q1) but the pace doesn’t last — by Q4, Pro is +0.227s/lap faster. HL Hero’s early speed comes at the cost of tire life. Other telemetry software would call HL Hero the faster driver based on best laps — but across the full 25-lap run, Pro’s sustainable approach wins by +1.111s.

The primary coaching theme is tire preservation. HL Hero is +0.249s/lap faster in Q1, but the advantage disappears — by Q4, Pro is +0.227s/lap faster. This +0.476s swing is driven by RF temperatures running 65°F hotter. The per-corner recommendations below are the specific inputs generating that excess heat. Fix the cause and the late-run fade (+0.465s worse) fixes itself.

Pro’s approach: set up the exit. Pro sacrifices +0.089s/lap on entry to gain +0.055s/lap on exit — a more patient entry produces a better run off the corner.

1. T1&T2

HL Hero is +0.015s/lap faster here, but the aggressive inputs are contributing to tire wear.

Pro lifts earlier (about 106 ft earlier) — 3.8% vs 5.9% of lap. On entry: HL Hero should use less wheel (35.0° vs 45.2°); HL Hero should start braking (16.7% vs 0.1% currently); HL Hero should carry less throttle (17% vs 31%). Pro gets back on the throttle significantly earlier (about 242 ft sooner) — 12.9% vs 17.6% of lap. This earlier reapply lets Pro build speed through the middle of the corner. Through centre: HL Hero should use less wheel (43.9° vs 58.5°); HL Hero should run a tighter line (~3 ft) (0% vs 12% of track); once on the throttle earlier, ease onto it more gradually (4.6%/s vs 6.7%/s). On exit: HL Hero should get on the throttle more aggressively (1.9%/s vs 0.8%/s); HL Hero should run a tighter line (~1 ft) (21% vs 26% of track).

HL Hero is running higher front tire temps at T1&T2 (225°F vs 179°F) and higher rear tire temps (226°F vs 204°F) — front heat driven by steering and peak steering. Front temps rise 39°F from Q1 to Q4.

2. T3&T4

HL Hero is +0.007s/lap faster here, but the aggressive inputs are contributing to tire wear.

Pro lifts earlier (about 104 ft earlier) — 53.3% vs 55.3% of lap. On entry: HL Hero should carry less throttle (34% vs 50%); HL Hero should start braking (15.3% vs 0.0% currently); HL Hero should use less wheel (34.0° vs 42.6°). Pro gets back on the throttle significantly earlier (about 269 ft sooner) — 60.1% vs 65.4% of lap. This earlier reapply lets Pro build speed through the middle of the corner. Through centre: HL Hero should use less wheel (38.4° vs 52.3°); HL Hero should carry a bit more throttle (60% vs 48%); HL Hero should run a tighter line (~2 ft) (2% vs 8% of track). On exit: HL Hero should run a tighter line (~2 ft) (13% vs 19% of track).

HL Hero is running higher front tire temps at T3&T4 (226°F vs 186°F) and higher rear tire temps (226°F vs 209°F) — front heat driven by steering. Front temps rise 36°F from Q1 to Q4.
