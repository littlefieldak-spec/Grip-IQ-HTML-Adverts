From input differences to the real cause.

Pace Trace doesn't just say "you spun." It traces the entry choices that loaded the centre, the centre line that loaded the exit, and the exit throttle that finally broke grip — so the fix points at the right phase.

Causal Chain
tighter entry line (~18 ft) + lifted later (~245 ft) → tighter mid-corner (~25 ft) → more throttle + throttle–steering overlap → oversteer at exit

The system does not just eyeball traces. It uses trained machine-learning models to identify repeatable driver patterns and connect them to tire-temperature behavior across the run.

Good fit?

Built for serious improvement, not dashboard clutter.

Pace Trace is for you if…

  • You want Pace Trace to automatically rank every input difference between two runs.
  • You want a measured approach to improvement: every claim checked across laps and tied to a specific input.
  • You care about race pace and tire life across a full stint, and you want to see exactly where each one breaks down.
  • You want coaching targets in feet, degrees, and percent of lap that you can take straight to the next practice session.

Pace Trace may not be for you if…

  • You want a live in-cockpit overlay comparing your last lap to one reference lap.
  • You'd rather read raw traces by feel and trust instinct over a verification step that checks whether a difference is real or a one-lap fluke.
  • You only care about one-lap qualifying pace — race pace, tire fade, and stint-level patterns aren't your priority.
  • You want general feedback from a faster teammate — “drive easier on entry” — without specific guidance based on actual telemetry across a stint.
FAQ

How it actually works.

The practical answers to the questions a serious driver hits before signing up.

How do I get the uploader?

Once you're in, you download a small Windows uploader. Point it at your iRacing telemetry folder once and it runs in the background from there — you never have to open it again.

How does my data get captured?

Automatically. The uploader watches your iRacing telemetry folder while you drive and sends every test, practice, and race session up as soon as it's written — nothing to export, nothing to remember.

How do I receive my coaching reports?

Your report appears in the GripIQ dashboard after the comparison is processed. It includes the big-picture pattern, per-corner targets, and progress benchmarks. A complete coaching narrative and additional details aimed at increasing long-run speed are also included.

Who can I compare to?

Three options: yourself (old setup vs new, before vs after coaching, practice vs race), a teammate on the same car and track, or an anonymous benchmark driver from a higher iRating bracket. Same analysis engine regardless of who's on the other side — only the opponent changes.

Will you support more cars and series?

Early access is focused on iRacing oval racing, starting with the most-used oval cars and tracks. We are expanding coverage based on beta-driver demand.

Is my data private?

Yes — private by default. Your runs are visible only to you. Your setups stay private too — commercial setups are never displayed. A teammate can only see runs you've explicitly shared with them, and benchmark drivers in the anonymous pool stay anonymous. You can pull your data out of either at any time.

Can I get more details beyond the automated report?

Yes — advanced users have access to 150+ engineered telemetry channels recorded across the run, with detailed information on car handling, and can request custom channels.

What does it cost?

Free during early access. Pricing tiers will be announced before public launch, and early-access members get first access at a discounted rate.
